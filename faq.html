Epstein Files Discharge Petition - Facts, Sources, and Documentation

Quick Answers for Staff Calls

Q: Won't this expose victims?
A: No. The framework includes strict protection of survivor identities and CSAM, with allowances for ongoing prosecutions—but NOT redactions to spare reputations.
Q: Aren't the documents already released?
A: The recent dump was 97% duplicative. The petition forces a full, searchable release so the public can see what remains.
Q: Why a discharge petition?
A: It's a procedural safeguard that brings measures to the floor when leadership stalls. It's rules-based and neutral.
Q: Is this partisan?
A: Survivors stood beside members from both parties. Nancy Mace (R), MTG (R), and Boebert (R) have signed alongside Democrats.
Q: What's the redaction standard?
A: Protect survivors and active cases. No redactions for embarrassment.

About the Discharge Petition

What is a discharge petition?

A discharge petition is a procedural tool in the U.S. House of Representatives that allows members to force a floor vote on a bill that's been stuck in committee. It requires 218 signatures (a majority of the House) to succeed.

Why is this approach being used?

House leadership has not brought the Epstein files disclosure bill to a floor vote. The discharge petition bypasses leadership and forces a vote if enough members sign.

Current Status (September 2025)

  • 134 signatures secured out of 218 needed
  • 4 Republicans have signed (Massie, Mace, Greene, Boebert)
  • Need 84 more signatures to force a vote

About the Files

What's been released so far?

On September 3, 2025, the House Oversight Committee released ~33,000 pages, but:

  • Most were already public
  • Heavily redacted
  • 97% of content was either previously released or blacked out

What files remain unreleased?

  • Department of Justice investigation files
  • FBI interview records
  • Financial transaction records
  • Communications between prosecutors and Epstein's legal team
  • Records related to the 2008 non-prosecution agreement

Why weren't perpetrators prosecuted?

Marina Lacerda: "Our government could have saved so many women, but Jeffrey Epstein was too important and those women didn't matter"

The Proposed Framework

What's in the JFK-style disclosure framework?

  1. Presumption of release - default to transparency
  2. Narrow exemptions only for ongoing investigations and national security
  3. Independent review board including survivor advocate seat
  4. Automatic protections for minor identities and re-identifying details
  5. Public accountability with log of withheld documents
