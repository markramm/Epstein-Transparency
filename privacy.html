Privacy & Safety Policy

Our Commitment to Survivors

This tracker exists to support survivors' calls for transparency while protecting their privacy and dignity. We follow the same principles outlined in H.R. 4405 for victim protection.

Core Privacy Principles

1. Automatic Protection of Minors

We never publish, share, or speculate about the identities of any individuals who were minors at the time of alleged crimes. This mirrors the automatic redaction provisions in H.R. 4405.

2. Adult Survivor Choice

We only reference adult survivors who have chosen to speak publicly. We respect the opt-in principle: survivors control their own narratives.

3. No Doxxing or Harassment

We provide official office phone numbers only. We never share personal information, home addresses, or private contact details of any representatives or individuals.

4. Trauma-Informed Language

We use respectful, non-sensational language when discussing trafficking and abuse. We prioritize survivor dignity over engagement metrics.

Safety Guidelines for Advocates

When contacting representatives or sharing information:

  • Be respectful: Staff members are doing their jobs. Harassment hurts the cause.
  • Focus on process: Talk about the discharge petition and framework, not speculation.
  • Protect victims: Never share unverified claims about victims or perpetrators.
  • Use official channels: Only call official office numbers during business hours.
  • Document professionally: Report responses factually without personal attacks.

⚠️ Important Warning

Never attempt to:

  • Contact representatives at their homes
  • Share conspiracy theories or unverified claims
  • Name alleged victims who haven't come forward publicly
  • Engage in or encourage harassment of any kind
  • Use violent or threatening language

Such actions harm survivors and damage the legitimate push for transparency.

Data We Collect

This website collects no personal data from visitors. We do not use cookies, analytics, or tracking of any kind. The site is static HTML/CSS/JavaScript hosted on GitHub Pages.

When you report information via GitHub Issues:

  • Your GitHub username will be visible
  • We only use submitted information to verify and update the tracker
  • We never share contributor information beyond what's public on GitHub

Content Moderation

We reserve the right to:

  • Remove any content that violates victim privacy
  • Reject unverified or speculative claims
  • Block users who engage in harassment
  • Report threats to appropriate authorities

Legal Compliance

This tracker operates within all applicable laws:

  • We respect copyright and fair use principles
  • We comply with all privacy regulations
  • We do not encourage or facilitate illegal activity
  • We cooperate with legitimate law enforcement requests

Contact for Safety Concerns

If you see content that violates these policies or endangers someone's safety:

  • Report immediately via GitHub Issues
  • Mark the issue as "Safety Concern" in the title
  • We review safety reports within hours

Remember

The goal is transparency with accountability, not vigilantism. We support survivors by respecting their privacy choices and focusing on systemic change through democratic processes.

This policy aligns with the victim protection provisions in H.R. 4405
